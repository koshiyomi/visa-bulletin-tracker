Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Priority Date?

Your "place in line." It's the date the government received your immigration petition (e.g., I-130 or I-140/PERM). You must wait until your priority date is "current" to apply for a Green Card.

Final Action (Table A) vs Filing (Table B)?

Table A dictates when an actual Green Card can be approved and issued. Table B dictates when you are allowed to submit your I-485 application (if USCIS accepts Table B for that month).

What is Retrogression?

Sometimes the demand for visas exceeds the annual supply limit. When this happens, the government moves the cut-off dates backward (retrogresses) to slow down applications.

What do "C" and "U" mean?

C (Current): There is no backlog. Anyone in this category can apply immediately.
U (Unavailable): No visas are available right now, usually because the annual cap has been hit.
